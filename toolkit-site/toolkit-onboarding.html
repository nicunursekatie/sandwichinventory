📖 Sandwich Making Guides

Learn the proper techniques for making sandwiches. Review the key points below, then view the full guide(s) for your event type. You must view at least one guide to continue.

🥪

Deli Sandwich Key Points

  • Each sandwich uses 2 slices of cheese and 2-3 slices of deli meat (turkey) on white or white wheat bread
  • No condiments — sandwiches are made with meat, cheese, and bread only
  • The specific assembly order protects the bread from moisture and prevents sogginess
  • After assembly, each sandwich goes into an individual sandwich baggie
  • Bagged sandwiches are stacked back inside the loaf bag the bread came from for transport

PB&J Sandwich Key Points

  • Use 1 tbsp of peanut butter on one slice of bread, 2 tbsp on the other
  • Add 1.5 tbsp of jelly on one slice — not too close to the edges to prevent mess
  • Keeping jelly away from edges ensures sandwiches stay neat during transport and eating
  • After assembly, each sandwich goes into an individual sandwich baggie
  • Bagged sandwiches are stacked back inside the loaf bag the bread came from for transport

🛡️ Food Safety Requirements

Food safety is critical when preparing food for donation. Review these key requirements, then view the full guide. You must view this document to continue.

🧼

Before You Start — All Sandwich Types

  • Wash hands with soap and water — hand sanitizer is NOT sufficient
  • Tie hair back or wear hair nets and beard nets
  • Put away phones and other personal belongings
  • Clean counter tops and cover with disposable plastic table cloths for easy cleanup
  • Put on food-safe gloves after setup is complete
🥩

Deli Sandwich Food Safety

  • Only take out one package of meat and cheese at a time (or one per table)
  • Keep all other meat and cheese refrigerated until the moment it's needed — don't let it sit out while assembling hundreds of sandwiches
  • After each loaf of sandwiches is assembled, return them to the refrigerator to cool before transport
  • For events making ~200+ sandwiches, TSP will send a driver with coolers and ice packs to pick up your sandwiches!
🥜

PB&J Food Safety

  • Use separate knives/spreaders for peanut butter and jelly — never mix utensils
  • This prevents cross-contamination and keeps jars clean for future use
