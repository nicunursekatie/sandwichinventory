Assembly Process

1 Lay Out Bread

  1. Everyone spreads around table/counter
  2. Lay bread slices side by side until workspace is filled
  3. Remove and discard ends/heels
  4. Count slices (2 slices = 1 sandwich)
Why: Counting now prevents confusion later

2 Add Cheese

  1. Place ONE slice of cheese on EACH piece of bread
  2. Work systematically left to right
  3. Call out any bread without cheese
Why: Every sandwich needs exactly 2 cheese slices total

3 Add Meat

  1. Check your portion plan (see options below)
  2. Work systematically to avoid missing pieces
  3. Keep track of which pieces have 1 vs 2 slices
2 Slices Total: 1 slice on EACH piece
3 Slices Total: 1 slice on half, 2 on other half

4 Close & Bag Singles

  1. Match halves carefully (especially if using 1+2 meat method)
  2. Close sandwiches
  3. Place each in individual zip-lock bag
  4. Press out excess air and seal tightly

5 Pack & Refrigerate

  1. Place bagged sandwiches into loaf bags
  2. Secure with twist tie (NO knots or rubber bands)
  3. Add correct label (Deli or PB&J)
  4. IMMEDIATELY to refrigerator
Why: Every minute counts toward the 2-hour safety limit

Batch Guidelines

Start Small: First-time groups should start with a small batch (20-30 sandwiches) to establish your pace.

Key Rule: Only prep what you can complete within the safety window. Better to do multiple small batches than risk food safety with one large batch.
Remember: Complete current batch FULLY before starting next batch. Never have two batches out at once.
