Group Event Assembly Guide

The Sandwich Project - For Event Organizers

The Assembly Line Method

How It Works

The entire group works on one stage at a time, filling your workspace with bread before moving to the next step together. This ensures consistency and food safety while making the process fun and efficient for volunteers.

Stage 1: Lay Out Bread

  • Everyone spreads out around your table/counter
  • Lay out bread slices side by side until workspace is filled
  • No ends/heels
  • Count your slices to track sandwich count

Stage 2: Add Cheese

  • Everyone adds one slice of cheese to EACH piece of bread
  • Work systematically so no bread gets missed
  • Call out if you notice any bread without cheese

Stage 3: Add Meat

  • Everyone adds meat based on your portion plan

For 2 Slices Total

Put 1 slice on EACH piece of bread/cheese. When closed, each sandwich will have 2 slices.

For 3 Slices Total

Put 1 slice on half the pieces, 2 slices on the other half. Carefully match them when closing for 3 slices per sandwich.

Stage 4: Close & Bag

  • Match up halves and close sandwiches
  • Each sandwich goes into individual zip-lock bag
  • Press out excess air, seal tightly

Stage 5: Pack Loaves

  • Place bagged sandwiches into loaf bags
  • Secure with twist tie (no knots or bands)
  • IMMEDIATELY to refrigerator

Critical Rules for This Method

The Two-Hour Rule

Batch Sizing

Safety First: Only lay out as much bread as you can complete in 20 minutes
  • Better to do multiple smaller batches than risk food safety
  • Typical safe batch: 20-40 sandwiches depending on group size and experience
  • First-time groups: start with 20 sandwiches to establish your pace

Designated Runner Role

Assign one person who DOESN'T participate in assembly. This person:

  • Brings out only enough meat/cheese for current batch
  • Returns completed sandwiches to refrigeration immediately
  • Tracks time and calls out warnings at 15 and 20 minutes
  • Prepares supplies for next batch while current batch is being assembled
  • Ensures everyone has gloves and replaces them as needed

Never Break These Rules

  1. Complete batches fully - Don't start new bread until current batch is refrigerated
  2. 20-minute maximum - From meat out to sandwiches refrigerated
  3. Runner controls the fridge - No one else opens it
  4. No accumulation - Sandwiches don't sit waiting to be bagged
